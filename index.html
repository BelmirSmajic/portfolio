Project 01

Healthcare Analytics

Provider Cost Outlier Analysis

Normalized provider cost per active member month within peer groups for a regional Medicaid health plan, helping Finance and Provider Contracting focus on materially abnormal cost patterns.

Business at a Glance

Industry and setting
Regional Medicaid health plan and healthcare payer analytics.
Who the work supported
Finance, Provider Contracting, and business users reviewing provider cost variation.
Business problem
Total provider spending alone did not show whether a provider was genuinely high cost because providers differed in member volume, specialty, and active exposure.
Why it was useful
The analysis gave business users a defensible way to focus investigation on providers materially above their peer median.
Key Impact
Supported more than $500,000 in documented annual healthcare cost reductions through targeted provider and policy review.
Tools
SQL and Tableau.

What I Owned

  • Gathered and translated business requirements
  • Defined relevant provider peer groups
  • Established the normalized cost methodology
  • Investigated service and billing drivers behind flagged providers

Provider Review Workspace

Six examples show Cost per active member month, peer median, above median amount, ratio, review status, and active member volume.

Example data: provider peer review dataset

Tools and Methods

Tools
SQL, Tableau
Methods
Claims to provider joins, member provider grain aggregation, provider level rollup, Cost per active member month, Peer group comparison, Provider outlier ranking, Contracting analytics review

  • Compared projected savings with actual results

Project 03

Data Integration and Product Analytics

Acquisition Product Matching

Designed matching logic and exception workflow for a Fortune 500 food distribution company comparing approximately 1.4 million acquired products with roughly 4 million existing products.

Business at a Glance

Industry and setting
Fortune 500 food distribution company integrating a large acquired product catalog into an existing enterprise product master.
Who the work supported
Product data, acquisition integration, operations, reporting, and business users responsible for resolving uncertain product matches.
Business problem
People had to manually compare products across large Excel spreadsheets where descriptions, manufacturers, brands, package sizes, units, quantities, abbreviations, and identifiers did not always align.
Why it was useful
The workflow surfaced likely matches, automatically resolved strong cases, preserved human review for uncertain records, and saved hundreds of manual work hours.
Key Impact
Saved hundreds of manual work hours while preserving focused review for uncertain or high risk records.
Tools
Python and Excel.

What I Owned

  • Translated the integration problem into matching requirements
  • Defined exact and fuzzy matching rules
  • Established confidence categories and review thresholds
  • Tested false positive and false negative scenarios

Python, Excel

Project 05

Independent Football Analytics Research

World Cup Weakest Link Analysis

Tested whether a team’s weakest regular starters were more informative about group stage performance and advancement than total team value or star power among the tested value measures.

Research at a Glance

Research setting
Independent football analytics research using public tournament usage, player valuation, and team performance data.
Purpose and audience
A public research project designed to test and communicate a football analytics hypothesis.
Research question
Does lineup floor track group stage performance and advancement better than total value or star power?
Why it matters
Football analysis often emphasizes headline talent while underweighting weakness among regular starters.
Key Finding
Lineup floor had the strongest observed relationship among the tested value measures, including a 0.77 Spearman relationship with group stage points.
Tools
Python, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, GitHub Pages.

What I Owned

  • Defined the lineup floor research question
  • Constructed the usage based Core XI
  • Joined player values and group stage results
  • Compared lineup floor, total value, and star power

The Core XI is retrospective, based on group stage minutes, starts, and appearances. The bottom three are the three lowest valued players within that Core XI, not the full squad and not every player who appeared.

Tools

Methods
Data cleaning, usage based Core XI construction, player valuation analysis, Spearman correlation, comparative team analysis
