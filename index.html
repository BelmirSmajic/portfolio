Professional Healthcare Analytics Project

Provider Cost Outlier Analysis

Developed a peer based provider cost analysis for a regional Medicaid health plan to distinguish genuine cost outliers from providers whose spending reflected specialty, volume, or member exposure.

Supported more than $500,000 in documented annual healthcare cost reductions by querying 12 months of claims data in SQL, calculating provider cost per active member month, and ranking peer group outliers in Tableau for targeted provider and policy review.

Business at a Glance

Industry and setting
Regional Medicaid health plan and healthcare payer analytics.
Who the work supported
Finance, Provider Contracting, and business users reviewing provider cost variation.
Business problem
Total provider spending alone did not show whether a provider was genuinely high cost because providers differed in member volume, specialty, and active exposure.
Why the work was useful
The analysis gave business users a defensible way to focus investigation on providers materially above their peer median.
Google XYZ achievement
Supported more than $500,000 in documented annual healthcare cost reductions by querying 12 months of claims data in SQL, calculating provider cost per active member month, and ranking peer group outliers in Tableau for targeted provider and policy review.
Tools used
SQL, Tableau, claims analysis, provider and member data, provider peer grouping, cost per active member month, peer ranking, root cause analysis, stakeholder validation.

Business Context

Raw provider spending did not reliably show which providers were truly abnormal. Cost per active member month normalized spending by exposure, then compared providers within relevant peer groups.

What I Owned

  • Gathered and translated business requirements
  • Defined relevant provider peer groups
  • Established the normalized cost methodology
  • Investigated service and billing drivers behind flagged providers
  • Built repeatable reporting for business review

Result or Business Value

The work supported provider and policy actions that generated more than $500,000 in documented annual healthcare cost reductions and gave stakeholders a repeatable review method.

Professional Healthcare Product and Contract Strategy Project

Preferred Provider Contract Scenario Model

Modeled reimbursement and volume shift scenarios for a proposed preferred durable medical equipment provider arrangement at a regional Medicaid health plan, then compared projected savings with actual post implementation performance.

Supported a preferred provider contract decision by using SQL claims analysis and scenario modeling to compare eligible services, reimbursement rates, historical utilization, and expected volume shift for a specific DME provider, then validating projected savings against post implementation performance.

Business at a Glance

Industry and setting
Regional Medicaid health plan and durable medical equipment contracting.
Who the work supported
Finance, Provider Contracting, and operational stakeholders evaluating a proposed preferred provider arrangement.
Business problem
Stakeholders needed to know whether shifting eligible durable medical equipment volume to a specific DME provider would generate savings.
Why the work was useful
The model tested whether the specific provider arrangement was financially attractive and showed whether projected savings materialized after implementation.
Google XYZ achievement
Supported a preferred provider contract decision by using SQL claims analysis and scenario modeling to compare eligible services, reimbursement rates, historical utilization, and expected volume shift for a specific DME provider, then validating projected savings against post implementation performance.
Tools used
SQL claims analysis, Excel scenario modeling, CPT and HCPCS service analysis, rate comparison, historical utilization analysis, volume shift modeling, projected savings analysis, post implementation validation.

Business Context

The decision required more than comparing two rate schedules. Savings depended on eligible CPT and HCPCS services, historical utilization, current reimbursement, benchmark reimbursement, provider rate structure, expected volume shift, and implementation assumptions.

What I Owned

  • Clarified eligible service and code scope
  • Documented reimbursement and volume assumptions
  • Modeled alternative rate and steerage scenarios
  • Aligned Finance, Contracting, and operational stakeholders
  • Compared projected savings with actual results

The model gave stakeholders a structured way to test a specific provider arrangement before making a decision and to evaluate whether savings materialized after implementation.

Professional Data Integration and Product Project

Acquisition Product Matching

Designed matching logic and exception workflow for a Fortune 500 food distribution company integrating a large acquired product catalog into an existing enterprise product master.

Saved hundreds of manual work hours by using Python and a structured matching workflow to compare approximately 1.4 million acquired products with roughly 4 million existing products, automatically resolve strong matches, and route uncertain records for focused human review.

Business at a Glance

Industry and setting
Fortune 500 food distribution company integrating a large acquired product catalog into an existing enterprise product master.
Who the work supported
Product data, acquisition integration, operations, reporting, and business users responsible for resolving uncertain product matches.
Business problem
People had to manually compare products across large Excel spreadsheets where descriptions, manufacturers, brands, package sizes, units, quantities, abbreviations, and identifiers did not always align.
Why the work was useful
The workflow surfaced likely matches, automatically resolved strong cases, preserved human review for uncertain records, and saved hundreds of manual work hours.
Google XYZ achievement
Saved hundreds of manual work hours by using Python and a structured matching workflow to compare approximately 1.4 million acquired products with roughly 4 million existing products, automatically resolve strong matches, and route uncertain records for focused human review.
Tools used
Python, Excel source files, Excel review outputs, data profiling, text normalization, exact matching, fuzzy matching, confidence scoring, exception handling, manual review workflow, validation of false matches, validation of missed matches.

Business Context

The acquired and existing catalogs represented similar products differently. At the scale of millions of records, spreadsheet search and manual comparison were not practical or reliable.

What I Owned

  • Translated the integration problem into matching requirements
  • Defined exact and fuzzy matching rules
  • Established confidence categories and review thresholds
  • Designed exception categories for uncertain matches
  • Tested false positive and false negative scenarios

Result or Business Value

The workflow saved hundreds of manual work hours while preserving human review for uncertain or high risk records.

Independent Data Research Project

World Cup Weakest Link Analysis

Tested whether the quality of a team’s weakest regular starters explained World Cup group stage performance better than total team value or top end star power.

Identified a 0.77 Spearman relationship between lineup floor strength and group stage performance by constructing a usage based Core XI for each team and comparing the three lowest valued regular starters with results, advancement, total value, and star power.

Business at a Glance

Industry and setting
Independent football analytics research using public tournament usage, player valuation, and team performance data.
Who the work supported
An independent research project designed to test a football analytics hypothesis and communicate the finding publicly.
Business problem
Football analysis often emphasizes total team value and elite players while underweighting weakness among regular starters.
Why the work was useful
The analysis created a repeatable way to measure lineup floor strength and showed how weak regular starters can reveal competitive limitations.
Google XYZ achievement
Identified a 0.77 Spearman relationship between lineup floor strength and group stage performance by constructing a retrospective usage based Core XI for each team and comparing the three lowest valued regular starters with results, advancement, total team value, and star power.
Tools used
Python, data cleaning, data validation, statistical analysis, Spearman correlation, public football data, public player valuation data, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, GitHub Pages.

Research Context

The Core XI is retrospective, based on group stage minutes, starts, and appearances. The bottom three are the three lowest valued players within that Core XI, not the full squad and not every player who appeared.

What I Owned

  • Defined the lineup floor research question
  • Constructed the usage based Core XI
  • Joined player values and group stage results
  • Validated complete team and player coverage
  • Compared lineup floor, total value, and star power

Result or Business Value

The analysis found that lineup floor had the strongest observed relationship with group stage performance and advancement among the tested value measures.
