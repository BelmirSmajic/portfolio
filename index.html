Professional Healthcare Analytics Project

Provider Cost Outlier Analysis

Developed a peer based provider cost analysis that distinguished genuinely high cost providers from providers whose spending appeared high because of specialty, volume, or member exposure.

Supported more than $500,000 in documented annual healthcare cost reductions by building normalized provider cost benchmarks, identifying peer group outliers, and translating findings into targeted provider and policy reviews.

Business Context

Raw provider spending did not reliably show which providers were truly abnormal. High spending could reflect member volume, specialty, active time, or case mix.

What I Owned

  • Gathered and translated business requirements
  • Defined relevant provider peer groups
  • Established the normalized cost methodology
  • Investigated service and billing drivers behind flagged providers
  • Built repeatable reporting for business review

Result or Business Value

The work supported provider and policy actions that generated more than $500,000 in documented annual healthcare cost reductions.

Modeled reimbursement and volume shift scenarios for a proposed preferred durable medical equipment provider arrangement, then compared projected savings with actual post implementation performance.

Business Context

What I Owned

  • Modeled alternative rate and steerage scenarios
  • Aligned Finance, Contracting, and operational stakeholders
  • Compared projected savings with actual results

Result or Business Value

The model gave stakeholders a structured way to test contract assumptions before making a decision and to evaluate whether savings were materializing after implementation.

Professional Data Integration and Product Project

Acquisition Product Matching

Designed the matching logic and exception workflow used to integrate approximately 146,000 acquired product records with an existing catalog of roughly 400,000 products.

Reduced manual product review by more than 50 percent by defining exact and fuzzy matching rules, confidence thresholds, and exception workflows for approximately 146,000 acquired products matched against roughly 400,000 existing products.

Business Context

The acquired and existing catalogs represented similar products differently. Incorrect matches could create duplicate products and downstream operational or reporting problems.

What I Owned

  • Translated the integration problem into matching requirements
  • Defined exact and fuzzy matching rules
  • Established confidence categories and review thresholds
  • Designed exception categories for uncertain matches
  • Tested false positive and false negative scenarios

Result or Business Value

The workflow reduced manual product review by more than 50 percent while preserving human review for uncertain or high risk records.

Independent Data Research Project

World Cup Weakest Link Analysis

Tested whether the quality of a team’s weakest regular starters explained World Cup group stage performance better than total squad value or top end star power.

Identified a 0.77 Spearman relationship between lineup floor strength and group stage performance by constructing a usage based Core XI for each team and comparing the three lowest valued regular starters with results, advancement, total value, and star power.

Research Context

The Core XI is retrospective. It is based on group stage minutes, starts, and appearances. The bottom three are the three lowest valued players within that Core XI.

What I Owned

  • Defined the lineup floor research question
  • Constructed the usage based Core XI
  • Joined player values and group stage results
  • Validated complete team and player coverage
  • Compared lineup floor, total value, and star power

Result or Business Value

The analysis found that lineup floor had the strongest observed relationship with group stage points among the tested value measures.
